One aspect of web browsers I didn't know nearly as much about was URL's and their prefixes. The most common prefix, HTTP, or hypertext transfer protocol, transmits HTML(hypertext markup langauge) webpages, which can supply almost any kind of content, including text, images, video, and applications. HTTPS is simply a more secure version of HTTP. One thing I learned is that www is not a mandatory part of webpages, its simply such a ubiquitous internet application that many web browsers simply assume that the webpage you are accessing is a part of the world wide web.
